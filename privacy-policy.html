OneLens Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 20 January 2026

1. Introduction

OneLens is an iOS camera application developed by BrunchDevelopment. This Privacy Policy explains how OneLens handles your data when you use the app and connect it to a cloud storage provider.

OneLens is designed to upload your photos and videos directly from your device to the cloud provider(s) you choose. OneLens does not run its own servers for storing your media. Your content is sent only to the cloud provider(s) you explicitly authorize, and only minimal information is stored locally on your device as described in this policy.

2. Connected Cloud Providers

OneLens allows you to connect one or more cloud storage providers, such as:

  • Google Drive
  • Google Photos
  • Microsoft OneDrive

The rules described in this Privacy Policy apply regardless of which provider you connect. Access is granted only after explicit consent via the OAuth consent screen. You can disconnect any provider at any time. When disconnected, OneLens removes locally stored thumbnails, cached data, pending uploads, and stored authentication tokens related to that provider. Media already uploaded to the cloud provider remains stored with that provider and is governed by its privacy policy.

3. Data We Access

OneLens accesses only the data needed to perform actions you request, such as uploading or viewing your photos and videos. The app can only access content created by OneLens.

For connected cloud providers, OneLens may access:

  • An account identifier needed to sign you in and keep your session active
  • Photos and videos created using OneLens
  • Basic file information (such as filename, size, and upload time)
  • Media details stored in the file itself (such as EXIF data or GPS location, if enabled by you)
  • Technical upload status information needed to complete uploads
  • General storage usage information (for example, used versus available space)

OneLens cannot browse, view, or access files that were not created by the app and cannot see files you upload to the cloud outside of OneLens.

OneLens does not access:

  • Your email address
  • Your name or profile photo
  • Your contacts, calendars, or messages
  • Any files not created by OneLens

4. How We Use Data

User data is accessed solely to perform user-requested actions, such as uploading photos or videos to the connected cloud provider, displaying upload status, supporting resumable uploads where applicable, and displaying or deleting user-created content.

OneLens does not use your data for advertising, analytics, tracking, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to the explicit functionality requested by the user.

OneLens may offer optional, anonymized crash and bug reporting to improve app stability. These reports do not include your photos, videos, cloud account data, or personal information.

5. Local Processing and Storage

  • Pending uploads: Photos and videos waiting to be uploaded are stored temporarily on your device and are automatically deleted once the upload finishes.
  • Thumbnails: Small preview images may be stored on your device to make the app faster. You can turn this feature off at any time in the app settings.
  • Sign-in information: Cloud access tokens are stored securely using Apple’s iOS Keychain.

6. Data Sharing

OneLens shares your data only with the cloud provider(s) you choose.

  • Your data is never routed through OneLens servers
  • Your data is never sold or shared with unrelated third parties
  • Each cloud provider works independently under your authorization
  • Once uploaded, data is governed by that provider’s privacy policy.

OneLens uses Supabase only for optional, anonymized crash and bug reports. This feature is turned off by default. No cloud provider data, media files, or sign-in credentials are sent to Supabase.

7. Data Security

All communication between OneLens and connected cloud providers is encrypted using HTTPS with TLS. Data stored on your device is protected using standard iOS security features, including device encryption and secure key storage.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

OneLens does not store your cloud data on its own servers. You control all stored data.

You can remove your data at any time by:

  • Deleting the OneLens app
  • Signing out of or disconnecting a cloud provider
  • Removing the OneLens folder from “On My iPhone”
  • Turning off thumbnail storage in the app settings

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

If we make important changes to this Privacy Policy, we will inform you inside the app. Continued use of OneLens means you accept the updated policy.
